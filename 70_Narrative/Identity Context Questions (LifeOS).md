
# Identity Context Questions (LifeOS)

### How to use these

- Answer in your own words
    
- Bullet points are fine
    
- “I don’t know yet” is a valid answer
    
- Revisit no more than 1–2 times per year
    

---

## 1. Lived Reality (Who you actually are today)

1. What kinds of work do you _actually_ spend most of your time doing right now?
    AI-Assisted development of Raybox, LifeOS, etc. I am deep in a building phase of my life right now, and am spending a lot less time (if any) working on music, art, or other non-tech creative projects
2. What responsibilities do you currently carry, regardless of whether you chose them?
    
3. What constraints are non-negotiable in your life right now?  
    (health, money, location, caregiving, legal, energy)
    
4. What reliably drains your energy?
    
5. What reliably restores your energy?
    

---

## 2. Strengths You Can Rely On (Evidence-based)

6. What are you consistently good at _even when tired or stressed_?
    
7. What do other people reliably come to you for?
    
8. What kinds of problems do you notice before others do?
    
9. What skills have you demonstrated repeatedly, not just once?
    
10. What environments tend to bring out your best work?
    

---

## 3. Friction Patterns (Where things break)

11. In what situations do you predictably struggle or stall?
    
12. What kinds of tasks do you avoid even when they’re important?
    
13. What patterns tend to precede burnout for you?
    
14. What well-intentioned systems or advice have _not_ worked for you?
    
15. What kinds of pressure reliably backfire?
    

---

## 4. Values in Practice (Not ideals)

16. What do you consistently make time for, even when things are hard?
    
17. What do you tend to protect or preserve when tradeoffs are required?
    
18. What compromises are you _not_ willing to make anymore?
    
19. What kinds of work feel meaningful _after_ you’ve done them (not before)?
    
20. What outcomes tend to make effort feel “worth it” in hindsight?
    

---

## 5. Motivation & Drive (What actually moves you)

21. What pulls you forward more reliably: curiosity, obligation, urgency, or meaning?
    
22. What kinds of stakes make you show up?
    
23. What kinds of rewards actually work for you?
    
24. What makes you disengage or shut down?
    
25. What does “too much” feel like, and how do you usually know you’ve crossed it?
    

---

## 6. Relationship to Work & Creation

26. What role does creative work play in your life right now?
    
27. How do you tend to oscillate between exploration and execution?
    
28. What kind of autonomy do you need to do good work?
    
29. What kind of structure helps rather than hurts?
    
30. What does “finishing” usually feel like for you?
    

---

## 7. Directional Self-Understanding (Becoming, not identity)

31. In the last few years, what direction have you been moving _in practice_?
    
32. What kinds of situations are you trying to have _more_ of?
    
33. What kinds of situations are you trying to have _less_ of?
    
34. What patterns are you actively trying to change?
    
35. What does “progress” look like at your current stage of life?
    

---

## 8. Boundaries & Non-Goals (Critical for LifeOS)

36. What are you explicitly _not_ trying to optimize for?
    
37. What goals do you no longer want the system to push you toward?
    
38. What kinds of metrics make you anxious or distorted?
    
39. What would success _not_ be worth to you?
    
40. What does a “good enough” life look like right now?
    

---

## 9. Trust & Agency

41. When do you trust your own judgment most?
    
42. When do you tend to outsource judgment (and why)?
    
43. What helps you regain trust in yourself after a setback?
    
44. How do you usually know when something is aligned vs misaligned?
    
45. What role do you want LifeOS to play when you’re unsure?
    

---

## 10. Meta (Optional, but powerful)

46. What do you hope this system _never_ becomes?
    
47. What would make you stop using LifeOS?
    
48. If LifeOS worked well, what would change about your daily experience?
    
49. What kind of relationship do you want with this system over time?
    
50. What would “quiet success” look like for you?
    

---

## How LifeOS should use this (important reminder)

This section is:

- **read-only context**
    
- **never surfaced proactively**
    
- **never used to apply pressure**
    
- **never used to shame or motivate**
    

Its sole purpose is to:

- interpret ambiguity
    
- break ties gently
    
- explain recommendations when asked
