Finding a technical co-founder when you can’t offer a salary is challenging, but not impossible—especially if you already have a strong creative vision and some working prototypes, which you do with **Rheome**, **Mutax**, and **Phonoglyph**. Here’s a practical path you can start on right away:

---

### 1. Get Very Clear on the Offer
* **Compelling vision.** Draft a concise “why Rheome exists” document—what problem it solves, why it matters, and what the long-term opportunity looks like.  
* **Equity expectations.** Be ready to discuss a fair split. Early technical founders often expect meaningful equity (e.g., 30–50% depending on your own contributions and IP).  
* **Current assets.** Highlight what you’ve already built: your working prototypes, domain names, branding concepts, and audience in creative tech circles.

---

### 2. Network in the Right Rooms
* **Meetup & local events.** In San Francisco you’re surrounded by AI, hardware, and creative-tech meetups. Search for groups around music tech, interactive art, WebAudio, creative coding, or hardware prototyping.  
* **Hackathons & residencies.** Gray Area SF, Noisebridge, or art/tech hackathons attract exactly the sort of multi-disciplinary builders you need.  
* **University labs.** NYU ITP alumni networks, CCRMA (Stanford’s music/tech program), and SF State’s electronic music departments are fertile ground.

---

### 3. Lead with Shared Passion, Not “Free Labor”
People rarely join just for equity if the pitch sounds like “work for free.” Instead:
* Emphasize **co-ownership**: they shape the product and share upside.  
* Frame it as an **artistic + entrepreneurial adventure**, not just a job.  
* Show **momentum**: even small wins (press mentions, demos, early users) make it feel real.

---

### 4. Create Low-Barrier Collaboration
* Start with a **small experimental build** (e.g., a limited Mutax prototype or a new Phonoglyph effect) so a potential partner can taste the workflow before committing.  
* Use open, async-friendly tools (GitHub, Discord, Notion) so distance and time zones aren’t blockers.

---

### 5. Protect Both Sides
* **Founder agreement** early: outlines equity vesting (standard 4-year with 1-year cliff), IP ownership, decision-making, and exit scenarios.  
* Be transparent that you currently can’t provide cash compensation.

---

### 6. Keep Building Your Own Skills
Even if you find the right person, continuing to grow your technical chops strengthens the partnership and signals commitment.
